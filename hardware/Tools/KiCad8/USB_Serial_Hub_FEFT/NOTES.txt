====================================================================================================
USB Serial Hub - FE1.1S & FT232RL
====================================================================================================

WARNING : # DO NOT POWER FROM BOTH J3 (USB-to-Serial converter) and other sources simultaneously. By
            default, this module is powered either by VBus from the upstream USB port (J1) or by an
            external 5.3V power supply via J4, which serves as the primary supply. When an external
            power supply is connected, the P-MOSFETs will automatically disconnect VBus.
          # J4 can only be powered by a supply ranging from 5V to 5.5V. Using a 5.3V power supply is
            recommended due to the presence of Schottky diode D1.
          # If a different voltage is required for the USB-to-Serial converter, unbridge SB3 and
            bridge SB1. In this mode, Vcc at J3 can be powered by either a 3.3V or 5V supply. This
            supply is used exclusively for the USB-to-Serial converter's I/O voltage; therefore,
            a primary power supply must still be connected.

====================================================================================================

There are two major versions of the FE1.1S USB 2.0 high speed 4-port hub controller - revision A and
revision B. However, there might be intermediate undocumented versions as well.

Technically, revision B should be a drop-in replacement for revision A. However, inconsistencies
exist across different versions of the datasheets.

There seems to be no easy way to determine whether the IC is revision A or B simply by looking at
its markings.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

JP1 (PU-Sel)

    By default, this jumper is unbridged. If the IC's PWRJ pin is a push-pull output, leave the
    jumper unbridged. However, for revision B, the PWRJ pin should be open-drain. In this case,
    bridge the jumper to 3V3 (preferred) or 5V.

    Note that if LED D3 turns on sharply and turns off gradually when the hub is reset, then the
    PWRJ pin is very likely open-drain. When performing this test, J5 must be left unconnected;
    otherwise, the downstream device will always be powered, and LED D3 will always remain on.

JP2 (1V8), C2, and C5

    In revision A, pin 12 (VD18_O) serves as the 1.8V power output from the integrated regulator,
    while pin 28 (VD18) functions as the 1.8V power input. They should be connected together, and
    a 10uF decoupling capacitor is required.

    In revision B, pins 12 and 28 are backward compatible. However, it is strongly recommended to
    leave them as No-Connection in new designs. If one is using the revision B IC and wants to
    strictly follow the datasheet's recommendations, JP2 should be unbridged, with C2 and C5
    remaining unsoldered.

C3 and C1

    In revision A, pin 21 (VD33_O) serves as the 3.3V power output from the integrated regulator,
    while pin 13 (VD33) functions as the 3.3V power input. They should be connected together, and
    a 10uF decoupling capacitor is required.

    In revision B, pin 21 (VD33F) is used to attach a 3.3V output filter capacitor to the integrated
    regulator, whereas pin 13 is designated as No-Connection. However, the datasheet specifies that
    pin 13 can be kept connected to pin 21 for full backward compatibility; therefore, a jumper is
    not included in the design for this pin, and C3 and C1 must always be soldered.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Datasheets from various revisions state:
    "automatic re-enumeration when hub switches from self-powered mode to bus-powered mode"
However, they do not specify the reverse. This could imply that transitioning back to self-powered
mode is also automatic, or that the hub requires a reset to complete the re-enumeration. The hub
can be reset by pressing SW1.

Additionally, the USB-to-serial converter can be reset by pressing SW2.
